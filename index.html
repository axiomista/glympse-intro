What is Glympse?

Some of you likely traveled here via a ride-share service. The big ones (Lyft, Uber) deliver a solid, consistent, informative user experience. But what about other user experiences involving geospatial data?

That's where we come in for partners like AAA. Field services such as lawn care, home appointments, cable or appliance installation. Delivery of furniture or other ground-tracked items. Curbside or in-store pickup of food, groceries, other shopping. Most of our partners have a non-tech core competency and don't want to lag behind the times and customer expectations. Location sharing alleviates anxiety. And what kind of world is this that anyone waits for hours-- to have someone physically connect their cable, turn on and test their connection? We enable a full end-to-end location-sharing experience (called a “Glympse”) with ETAs, notifications, messaging, triggered phase changes, webhooks, reporting, privacy, feedback. We have IVR integration, a web experience, and mobile apps for Android and iOS. Then we white-label Glympses for enterprise customers. The service is private, simple at its core, and customizable. We try not to extend too far into the realm of platform, but we do integrate with many existing service management solutions like Descartes, SFDC, Dynamics, Oracle Field Service Connector, etc. Glympse services are also useful outside of the enterprise space: we support search-and-rescue by water or land and team coordination efforts for wilderness rescue groups and the Cajun Navy. We offer full pro-bono support to organizations who use us for humanitarian purposes. We leverage our SOA pretty extensively, have lots of distributed resources management, and use some great tools to get by with a small team. Some of you might remember the early days of Glympse as a consumer app. We were the first temporary real-time location sharing app, before Find My Friends or Messenger or any similar app functionality existed. The consumer app is still out there, although our development focus has been enterprise for a couple years now. As I see it there's still one major advantage to using Glympse over FMF/Messenger etc: Consumer privacy was then as now an emphasis of the consumer application. WE NEVER KEEP LOCATION DATA. IT’S THE WAY WE HAVE TECHNICALLY IMPLEMENTED OUR STACK. Check out our privacy policy! Our Biz Dev/Customer Success teams are always uncovering new use cases and potential integrations for our product. We are driving development as needed. Most of our partners are industry heavy-hitters; focus on Fortune 500. We are actively moving toward having all our services standardized in Go; Rewriting our core "glympse" service that manages the glympse object's invites and metadata improved performance and sold leadership on the initiative. Last year Zaq and I started hosting these fun voluntary R&D hacknights for producing POCs, working on pet projects, and fixing things that improve developer Quality of Life. Our team has varied work styles but we all enjoy ownership and challenges.

Why Go for us?

Small footprint


Collaborative codebase


Maximum readability and minimal overhead...

...with just the right amount of control under the covers

I don’t imagine I have to sell any of you on Go being a fun language to write code in. For Glympse specifically, we need to scale quickly alongside new customers, who bring in new volumes of traffic. We are productizing several new verticals right now. We’re a small team for the number of customers we support and for the size of our business impact. We try to keep our footprint low and respond rapidly to network traffic. FOR EXAMPLE, Pickup Service (BOPIS solution) has managed goroutines for push notifications to available agents. Imagine how the profile of that would compare to attempting async push with python and pubsubs... Maximized readability of code is great for teamwork. We require a well-designed, well-understood services architecture. We also need to control our overhead and costs. Our APIs can be hit hundreds or thousands of times for a single Glympse. This is where Go comes in for us: Maximum readability and minimal overhead, with just the right amount of control under the covers when we need it. Keeping syntax and overhead to a minimum means fast and fun building. We design and spin up services for a POC during a hacknight. Our collaboration is constant and energizing. Plus anyone who has worked with me knows I like typed languages; implicit conversions give me mild anxiety. We are really happy to have you all here.
